Who We Are

Yale Undergraduate Intelligent Vehicles (InVē) serves to stimulate and inspire top-notch engineering students at Yale with exciting autonomous robotics challenges. Each year, we dive into a collegiate competition that requires us to build a self-driving vehicle. In the process, students gain multidisciplinary technical skills, exposure to leading edge problems in engineering, and the opportunity to work with other like-minded peers in the development of new technologies.

Last year, InVē successfully entered a Segway-powered robot into the Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition. The inaugural team consisted of five members, each of whom had a hand in the mechanical, electrical, and software design of the system. Starting with funds from the Yale School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, we spent the Fall researching control algorithms for navigation, and the Spring designing and fabricating the vehicle chassis. Software and hardware came together in the months of April and May, and by the competition in June we competed well enough to win Rookie of the Year and 4th place in the Design Challenge.

This year, we have grown to ten members and have set upon building a robotic sailboat for entry into the International Robotic Sailing Regatta. In recruiting, we have selected students with diverse skillsets and a passion for creative problem solving. Our team is now stronger than ever, and we are eager to break ground in developing a robotic racing sailboat.

Using data and research collected from the model sailboat, we will design, architect, and construct an alpha version of the final sailboat design. Sensors will be attached to the prototype to test developed control algorithms.
